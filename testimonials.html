But ten minutes later, there was still no sign of the double-layered beefy goodness. I wasn’t leaving without my burger though, so I hunched myself down next to the counter like I was bracing for a snowstorm, and waited. At some point, I must’ve fallen asleep, because all I remember is seeing darkness all around me, except for a tiny pinprick of white. As I watched, the pinprick grew bigger and bigger, until I could see that it was actually a fuzzy ball of bright white light getting closer and closer. Eventually, I could make out the face of a small white dog in the centre of the light, and that the light was actually its fur, stretching out in all directions like a massive sea urchin. The dog looked at me solemnly, and even though its mouth didn’t move, I heard its voice inside my head. It spoke to me in a series of high-pitched barks and growls. I don’t know how I understood it, since I don’t speak dog, but somehow, I did. "You worship a false god", it ruffed.
